Usain Bolt

The fastest human of all time

Usain Bolt's life up to now:

  • 1986 - Born in Sherwood Content, a small town in Jamaica.
  • 2001 - Wins his first high school championship title in the 200 meters at the ISSA Championships.
  • 2002 - Becomes the youngest world junior gold medalist at the World Junior Championships in Kingston, Jamaica.
  • 2004 - Competes in his first Olympic Games in Athens, Greece, at the age of 18.
  • 2008 - Shatters the world records in the 100 meters, 200 meters, and 4x100 meters relay at the Beijing Olympics, becoming the first man since Carl Lewis in 1984 to win three gold medals in sprinting events at a single Olympics.
  • 2009 - Lowers his own 100 meters and 200 meters world records at the World Championships in Berlin, solidifying his status as the fastest man on the planet.
  • 2012 - Successfully defends his Olympic titles in the 100 meters, 200 meters, and 4x100 meters relay at the London Olympics.
  • 2013 - Retires from athletics briefly but returns to competitive sprinting the following year.
  • 2015 - Breaks his own 100 meters and 200 meters world records at the World Championships in Beijing.
  • 2016 - Participates in his final Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro, winning three gold medals in the 100 meters, 200 meters, and 4x100 meters relay.
  • 2017 - Participates in his final Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro, winning three gold medals in the 100 meters, 200 meters, and 4x100 meters relay.
  • 2020 - Named the "World Athlete of the Year" by World Athletics for the fourth time. Usain Bolt's extraordinary speed, charisma, and record-breaking achievements have left an indelible mark on the world of athletics. His dedication to the sport and his charismatic personality have made him not just a sports legend but a global icon. Bolt's impact goes beyond the track, inspiring aspiring athletes worldwide and showcasing the power of determination and passion.
